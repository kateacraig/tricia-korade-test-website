ABOUT

Meet Tricia

Tricia’s Story

Tricia Korade is a true independent running for Johnson City Commission, focused on local outcomes rather than partisan agendas. After a 35-year career in corporate finance and strategy, she has dedicated her semi-retirement to serving the community she is proud to call home.

Her career spans senior leadership roles at Citigroup, The Nielsen Company, and Accenture, where she managed operational budgets of more than $2 billion and led large, complex organizations. A certified Six Sigma problem-solver, she is completing a Master of Public Administration at ETSU, pairing decades of business experience with the academic rigor of public service.

Close to home, Tricia serves on the Washington County Board of Equalization and the Johnson City Senior Services Advisory Council, and she founded Welcoming Wellness, a nonprofit offering trauma-informed programs for veterans and first responders. Whether she is leading, volunteering, or simply listening, her focus stays the same: showing up in the community, for the community.

I am intent on not making campaign promises. I commit to only one thing: to focus on the issues that impact our community, making decisions that are people-informed and data-driven.

What Tricia brings

Strategic financial leadership

A 35-plus year career in corporate finance and Big Five consulting. Conservative-minded, she asks the tough questions and considers every viewpoint offered.

Business growth across all sectors

Keeping the Main Street America feel of our city means balancing investment in small businesses with larger industry.

Balancing the How, What, and Why

Good decisions are not only about the end point. Equally important is how we get there: listening to people, looking at data, and sharing why.

Community service

Servant leadership is a passion. Her semi-retirement is dedicated to supporting others with her time and talents.
